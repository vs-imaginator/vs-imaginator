Greetings digital traveler,

My name is Ernesto Matthew Zamudio, and this is my personal website. I’m a twenty-something with too many hobbies and not enough time. I have a nostalgic appreciation for retro tech, having many fond memories of using my family’s old Windows XP computer, which is where my passion for computer technology began. Some things I enjoy are being a hobbyist website developer, GNU/Linux tinkerer, dabble in programming whenever possible, and have been building computers for nearly a decade. I’m a lifelong learner and enjoy dipping my toes into as many topics as I am able to.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Computer System Technology at Illinois State University in the spring of ‘21. At university, in tandem with my major, I received a minor in East Asian Studies, with a focus on Japanese language.

Aside from computer related focuses, I am a self-proclaimed Japanophile and enjoy researching the Japanese language and culture, an interest I’ve held for a number of years. My primary areas of expertise are “Lost Decade” and researching the cultural impact of pop culture. I also fan-translate Japanese manga and song lyrics to further refine my language abilities. Additionally, I’m a book-lover and enjoy writing and researching essays across various blogs in my spare time.

Everything on this site was programmed by hand in the Atom text editor on my Lenovo Thinkpad x220 running Ubuntu 20.04.
